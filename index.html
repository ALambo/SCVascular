Conditions We Treat

Vein & Vascular Conditions

Varicose Veins

Twisted, enlarged veins often visible just under the skin—causing aching, heaviness, and swelling. Highly treatable with modern ablation techniques.

Spider Veins & Telangiectasias

Small, web-like veins near the skin surface. Sclerotherapy and laser treatment eliminate them effectively with minimal downtime.

Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT)

Blood clots in deep veins require urgent evaluation. We offer catheter-directed thrombolysis and mechanical thrombectomy for severe cases.

Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD)

Narrowed arteries reduce blood flow to the limbs causing pain and non-healing wounds. Endovascular stenting and bypass restore circulation.

Chronic Venous Insufficiency

When leg veins fail to return blood efficiently—causing swelling, skin changes, and ulcers. Ablation and compression therapy are first-line treatments.

Aortic Aneurysm (AAA/TAA)

Bulging of the aorta can be life-threatening if ruptures. We offer endovascular aneurysm repair (EVAR/TEVAR) as a minimally invasive alternative to open surgery.

Comprehensive Vein Treatment

Our vein specialists offer the complete range of treatments for varicose veins, spider veins, and chronic venous insufficiency—from in-office procedures to advanced ablation techniques.

  • Endovenous Laser Ablation (EVLA/EVLT) — Laser energy closes diseased veins with minimal discomfort
  • Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA / ClosureFast™) — Heat-based closure with excellent long-term results
  • Sclerotherapy — Chemical injection for spider veins and smaller varicosities
  • Varithena® Foam Sclerotherapy — FDA-approved foam for larger varicose veins
  • Microphlebectomy — Tiny punctures to remove bulging surface veins
  • Compression Therapy — Medical-grade compression as adjunct treatment

Geniculate Artery Embolization (GAE)

GAE is a breakthrough, minimally invasive procedure for patients with knee osteoarthritis pain—offering significant relief without surgery or joint replacement.

Using fluoroscopic guidance, a small catheter is advanced to the arteries supplying the inflamed knee lining (synovium). Tiny microspheres are delivered to reduce blood flow to the inflamed tissue, decreasing pain and inflammation.

  • Outpatient procedure — home same day
  • No general anesthesia required
  • Significant pain reduction in 70–80% of patients
  • Ideal for patients who are poor surgical candidates or prefer non-surgical options
  • Can be repeated if symptoms recur

Renal Denervation

Renal denervation is an innovative catheter-based treatment for resistant hypertension—high blood pressure that doesn't respond adequately to medications alone.

The procedure uses ultrasound or radiofrequency energy delivered through a catheter to disrupt the renal sympathetic nerves surrounding the renal arteries, reducing overactive nerve signals that contribute to elevated blood pressure.

  • FDA-cleared (Recor Medical Paradise™ system)
  • Treats resistant or difficult-to-control hypertension
  • Outpatient procedure — typically 45–60 minutes
  • No stents or implants left behind
  • Can reduce systolic BP by 8–10 mmHg on average

Prostatic Artery Embolization (PAE)

PAE is a minimally invasive, non-surgical alternative to TURP (transurethral resection of the prostate) for men with benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) causing urinary symptoms.

Tiny microspheres are delivered through a catheter to block blood supply to specific areas of the enlarged prostate, causing it to shrink and relieving obstructive urinary symptoms—while preserving sexual function.

  • No incision, no general anesthesia
  • Preserves erectile and ejaculatory function
  • 85–90% improvement in urinary symptoms
  • Same-day or overnight procedure
  • Suitable for large prostates not amenable to other therapies
  • Reduced risk vs. surgical alternatives

Uterine Artery Embolization (UAE)

UAE is a proven, uterus-preserving treatment for symptomatic uterine fibroids—offering an effective alternative to hysterectomy or myomectomy.

Under imaging guidance, a catheter is used to deliver tiny particles to the uterine arteries, cutting off blood supply to fibroids. Without blood supply, fibroids shrink and symptoms dramatically improve.

  • Preserves the uterus — no hysterectomy required
  • Treats all fibroids simultaneously regardless of number or location
  • 85–90% of patients report significant improvement in heavy bleeding
  • Short hospital stay (overnight) with return to work in 1–2 weeks
  • Eliminates heavy menstrual bleeding, pelvic pressure, and bulk symptoms
