Geniculate Artery Embolization (GAE)

GAE is a breakthrough minimally invasive procedure for patients with knee osteoarthritis pain — offering significant relief without surgery or joint replacement.

A small catheter delivers tiny microspheres to reduce blood flow to the inflamed knee lining (synovium), decreasing pain and inflammation.

  • Outpatient — home same day
  • No general anesthesia required
  • Significant pain reduction in 70–80% of patients
  • Ideal for patients who are poor surgical candidates or prefer non-surgical options
  • Can be repeated if symptoms recur
Non-Surgical Same-Day Discharge Minimal Recovery

Renal Denervation

An innovative catheter-based treatment for resistant hypertension — high blood pressure that doesn't respond adequately to medications alone.

Ultrasound or radiofrequency energy disrupts the renal sympathetic nerves surrounding the renal arteries, reducing overactive nerve signals that elevate blood pressure.

  • FDA-cleared (Recor Medical Paradise™ system)
  • Treats resistant or difficult-to-control hypertension
  • Outpatient procedure — typically 45–60 minutes
  • No stents or implants left behind
  • Can reduce systolic BP by 8–10 mmHg on average
FDA-Cleared No Implants Outpatient

Prostatic Artery Embolization (PAE)

A minimally invasive, non-surgical alternative for men with benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) causing urinary symptoms — without the risks of traditional surgery.

Tiny microspheres block blood supply to areas of the enlarged prostate, causing it to shrink and relieving obstructive urinary symptoms while preserving sexual function.

  • No incision, no general anesthesia
  • Preserves erectile and ejaculatory function
  • 85–90% improvement in urinary symptoms
  • Same-day or overnight procedure
  • Suitable for large prostates not amenable to other therapies
No Incision Sexual Function Preserved High Success Rate

Uterine Artery Embolization (UAE)

A proven, uterus-preserving treatment for symptomatic uterine fibroids — an effective alternative to hysterectomy or myomectomy with faster recovery.

Tiny particles delivered through a catheter cut off blood supply to fibroids, causing them to shrink and dramatically improving symptoms.

  • Preserves the uterus — no hysterectomy required
  • Treats all fibroids simultaneously regardless of number or location
  • 85–90% of patients report significant improvement in heavy bleeding
  • Short hospital stay with return to normal activity in 1–2 weeks
  • Proven therapy with 30+ years of clinical evidence
Uterus Preserved Treats All Fibroids Fast Recovery

Vein Health Spotlight

Understanding Varicose & Spider Veins

Do You Recognize These Signs?

Do you wear pants or long skirts to cover bulging, discolored veins? Is your sleep disrupted by achy legs? Do you avoid standing for long periods? You're not alone — and effective, minimally invasive treatment is available.

What Are Varicose Veins?

Varicose veins are enlarged, twisted veins most commonly found in the legs. They occur when the valves in your veins stop working properly, causing blood to flow backward and pool — stretching and bulging the vein wall. They may appear as blue or purple cords just under the skin, often surrounded by spider veins.

Risk Factors

Age (weakening valves)
Family genetics
Hormonal changes
Obesity / extra pressure
Prolonged standing / sitting
Pregnancy

Symptoms That Warrant Evaluation

  • Aching, heavy, or tired legs — especially at day's end
  • Throbbing, burning, or cramping in the legs
  • Swelling in the lower legs or ankles
  • Itching around the veins
  • Skin discoloration, hardening, or texture changes
  • Restless legs at night

Why treat now? Beyond cosmetics, untreated varicose veins can progress to chronic skin changes, non-healing ulcers, and blood clots (DVT). Early treatment is easier, safer, and typically covered by insurance.

Vein Treatment Options

Minimally Invasive Vein Treatments

Most procedures are performed in our office in 30–60 minutes, with patients walking out the same day. The Vein Clinic is located at Springfield Clinic 1st - 800 Building.

🔆

Endovenous Laser Therapy (EVLT)

Uses heat energy delivered by a thin laser fiber to close the diseased vein. Minimally invasive with a quick recovery — most patients return to normal activity the next day.

Outpatient · Local Anesthesia
💉

Sclerotherapy

A sclerosing medication is injected directly into spider veins and smaller varicosities, causing them to collapse and gradually fade. In-office with no downtime.

In-Office · No Downtime
🫧

Varithena® Foam Sclerotherapy

FDA-approved injectable foam for larger and more tortuous varicose veins. The foam displaces blood in the vein, causing the vein wall to seal shut.

FDA-Approved · In-Office
✂️

Ambulatory Phlebectomy

Bulging surface veins are removed through tiny punctures in the skin — so small, they require no stitches. Excellent cosmetic results with minimal scarring.

Same-Day · No Stitches
🧦

Compression Therapy

Medical-grade compression stockings improve venous blood return and reduce symptoms. Often prescribed as first-line therapy or used alongside other treatments.

First-Line · Non-Invasive
📡

Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA)

Controlled radiofrequency heat energy is applied along the length of the diseased vein, sealing it closed. Excellent long-term results for saphenous vein insufficiency.

Outpatient · Proven Results

What is PAD?

Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD) occurs when fatty plaque narrows the arteries carrying blood to your legs — causing pain, cramping, and poor healing.

1 in 20
adults over 50 has PAD
⚠ Warning Signs
  • Leg cramping or pain when walking that stops at rest
  • Leg numbness or weakness
  • Cold or discolored feet or toes
  • Slow-healing wounds or sores on legs or feet
  • Shiny skin or hair loss on legs
